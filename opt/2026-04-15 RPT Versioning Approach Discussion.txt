📒Meeting Outline

Versioning approach for RPT (UI/API configs and data model)

• Current status and pending decision
  The team has not started backend development for versioning; a design decision is pending between two approaches: keeping a single collection with full copies per release/version vs. maintaining deltas and comparing across collections/documents. Clarity is required before schema finalization and development proceed.

• Proposed approach (single collection, full snapshot per release)
  For each release, store a complete config snapshot in one MongoDB collection (e.g., RPT version history). Expected volume is low (≈20–30 versions/year; even at 100/year over 10 years ≈1,000 docs), making storage manageable. Benefits cited: simpler implementation, easier maintenance and tracking of added/removed features, straightforward UI show/hide control driven by config (no hardcoding).

• Alternative approach (delta-based comparison)
  Maintain changes only and compute differences by comparing old vs. new documents/collections. This would add comparison logic complexity and ongoing maintenance overhead. Performance impact is expected to be minor, but the team prefers to avoid complexity and potential long-term maintenance costs.

• Scope of config controlled by versioning
  Page-level and form-level UI behaviors (e.g., homepage columns, RPT tabs PDW visibility, field-level toggles like client name, BG definition, opportunity). The same collection can hold UI and API-related configs if needed to simplify consumption. No separate API-config collection is planned unless it clearly helps downstream teams.

• Key stakeholder concerns and justification preparation
  Anticipated questions (from Srini) include rationale for duplication when there are no schema/UI changes between versions, long-term data growth, and performance. Team’s justification: low data volume, simpler maintenance and auditing, predictable retrieval, and avoiding complex delta logic. Performance difference is not significant; the driver is maintainability and clarity.

Dashboard will use the GET endpoint to fetch all versions (currently shows all versions, not filtered by a single RPT ID).
