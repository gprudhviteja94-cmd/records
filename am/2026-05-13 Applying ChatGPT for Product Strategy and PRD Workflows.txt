📒Meeting Outline

Applying ChatGPT for Product Strategy and PRD Workflows

• Creating a structured strategy brief (steps 1–5, with guardrails)
  The team uses step-by-step prompting to generate a strategy brief for an Amex PM: 1) problem/opportunity, 2) target customer/segment, 3) value proposition/differentiators, plus citations to uploaded source docs. Guardrails prevent inventing customer evidence or market demand and ensure early hypotheses are not framed as final strategy.

• Saving and retrieving work in Projects
  After generating the brief, the output is saved to the project context via the folder-with-down-arrow control. Saved briefs appear under Sources, enabling future iteration. Users can start new conversations within the same project to build on prior work.

• Pressure testing strategy briefs
  A “product strategy analyst for Amex” workflow is used to: 1) analyze market and competitor context, 2) identify threats and opportunities, and 3) list key leadership questions. Additional research notes can be uploaded to ground the analysis; guardrails again prevent inference beyond sources.

Data Analysis with Structured Datasets

• Establishing data understanding (schema-first approach)
  When uploading CSV/Excel, first ask ChatGPT to summarize dataset and columns to confirm understanding (decode acronyms, semantics). This verification step reduces misinterpretation before deeper analysis.

• Step-by-step analysis flow
  Recommended flow: 1) identify trends/outliers, 2) form hypotheses, 3) recommend next analyses, 4) produce an executive takeaway. This scaffolding yields clearer, defensible insights.

• Iterating analyses and artifacts
  ChatGPT remembers context in-session, enabling selective edits and consistent re-runs in the same format. Outputs can be turned into polished artifacts for sharing.

PRD Generation, Review, and Maintenance

• From unstructured notes to review-ready PRD
  Jane demonstrates turning ideation-stage notes (e.g., mural content) into a structured PRD using an Amex-aligned template. ChatGPT prompts for audience/context and fills sections like vision, problem, goals, and functional requirements.

• Quality assurance against Amex rubric
  The PRD is “pressure tested” against the Amex rubric to identify improvement areas (e.g., strengthening non-functional requirements). Final accountability remains with the PM, who refines content with engineering/design/servicing partners.

• Managing product changes (e.g., pricing pivot)
  ChatGPT scans the PRD to find all impacted sections/flows (e.g., pricing references, POE engine) and proposes edits. Version change history is maintained. Exports are available as Word and Markdown for stakeholder review and collaboration.

Guardrails, Accuracy, and Workflow Choices

• Reducing hallucinations
  Configuration explicitly instructs: do not invent content; all outputs must be traceable to sources; flag speculative areas. PMs conduct careful end-to-end reviews and restructure sections as needed.

• Custom GPT vs. Project usage
  Custom GPTs are static (templates, rubrics). Projects are dynamic (growing artifacts, saved conversations/sources). You can “@” a GPT within a Project to combine structured workflows with persistent project context. Historic GPT-only chats cannot be retro-added to Projects; future work should use @-tagging inside Projects.

• Aggregating multi-meeting inputs
  Start with a lean PRD, insert placeholders for TBD sections (e.g., business rules), and use these to drive future agendas. Keeping the work within a Project helps track iterative progress over weeks.
