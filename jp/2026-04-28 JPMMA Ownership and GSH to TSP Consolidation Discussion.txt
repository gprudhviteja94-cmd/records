📒Meeting Outline

JPMMA ownership, notifications, and Common Services alignment

• JPMMA vs Common Services responsibilities
  JPMMA now owns alert/notification publishing previously done by Common Services; client notification delivery flows go through JPMMA.
• Client configuration ownership
  Client-specific preferences (recipients, email IDs, view/data customization) remain under Common Services via the Client Preference module, integrated with Access World.
• Ownership clarity and contacts
  Common Services stakeholders identified as Sumit and Savita for coordination on preferences and integration touchpoints.

GSH to TSP consolidation: scope, capabilities, and connectivity

• Program context and goal
  As part of JCH/JGSH commission and payment consolidation, replicate current GSH behaviors on TSP/DSP without requiring back-office changes; use new DSP services to mirror JCH connectivity.
• Core batching capability on TSP
  TSP currently supports single-beneficiary high-value payments; a redesigned batch framework is being built to support GSH-like batching for client parity.
• File format expansion
  DSP currently supports two formats; needs to expand to ~8–9 GSH formats. Technically these are variants of CSV or fixed-width, both already supported in DSP (e.g., ANG as CSV, GTI as fixed-width). ESB/payment model updates required to map new fields.
• Back-office extraction/connectivity
  Today DSP connects to WPI and CKF only; new adapters/connectivity needed (e.g., to LBBI/other BOs). Decision pending on routing via WPI vs direct TSP connectivity; prior leaning noted but final decision unclear.
• Back-office interface replication
  GACS-style connectivity and interfaces to back offices must be reproduced within TSP to preserve current JCH behaviors under the chosen “strategy trail” with minimal downstream impact.

Entitlements and approval matrix migration

• Centralization of approval rules
  JCH currently holds matrix approval rules locally; migration will centralize these under Admin/Entitlements.
• Client overlap and harmonization
  Some clients use both DSP (high value) and GSH (low value) with potentially different approval matrices; rules must be merged and harmonized during migration with clear client communication.
• Ownership and alignment
  Summits’ team (Admin/Entitlements) is aware and will lead migration planning within the GSCF program.

Delivery risks, resourcing, and prioritization

• Competing initiatives
  Parallel tracks include app/tech/UI modernization, consolidation, DSP market expansion, and regulatory items—creating resource contention and schedule risk.
• Willingness to re-prioritize
  Leadership open to pausing/repurposing efforts to protect critical path for GSH→TSP; follow-up discussion needed to make explicit trade-off decisions.
• Open ownership decision
  Unresolved decision on who builds certain DSP services/connectors (DSP vs WPI) impacts planning and timelines.

📋Overview

• JPMMA now publishes notifications; Common Services retains client preference/configuration via the Client Preference module integrated with Access World.
• TSP must deliver a robust batch framework to match GSH client expectations for batching.
• DSP to expand support to ~8–9 GSH file formats (all CSV/fixed-width variants) and update ESB/payment models to map new fields.
• New back-office connectivity/adapters are required beyond current WPI/CKF; decision pending on using WPI as a hub vs direct TSP connections (e.g., LBBI).
• GACS-like back-office connectivity patterns to be replicated in TSP to avoid changes in downstream systems.
• JCH approval matrices to be centralized under Admin/Entitlements; overlapping DSP/GSH clients require rule harmonization and client communication.
• Resource contention across modernization, consolidation, market expansion, and regulatory items is a key delivery risk; re-prioritization options to be discussed.
• Ownership for building certain services/connectors (DSP vs WPI) remains undecided and needs resolution.
